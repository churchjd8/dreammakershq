# Part I: The Founder's DNA
Source: "The Art of the Startup" by Jeff Church
Chapters: 1-4

## Key Stories & Anecdotes

### The Coca-Cola Call (Chapter 1)
- **Date:** 5:00 p.m. on July 3, 2018
- Three years earlier (2015), Coca-Cola had taken a **30% minority stake** in Suja
- This was the potential second step -- full acquisition into the world's largest beverage company
- 280 employees were waiting on the outcome
- Suja had been named the **fastest-growing CPG brand in the U.S.** and landed high on **Forbes' list of most promising brands**
- Jeff's key insight: "Awards don't get deals done. Financial statements do."
- "When a company like Coca-Cola evaluates you, they aren't buying hype; they're evaluating durability."

### Kilimanjaro Origin of "Babu" (Preface)
- Jeff was climbing Kilimanjaro with his kids
- Around day three, with oxygen thin and legs burning, kids started calling him "Babu" (BAH-boo) -- Swahili for Grandpa
- Earned partly for slow pace, partly for offering unsolicited advice on the trail
- The name stuck -- his grandkids now call him Babu

### Jeff's Background & Track Record (Chapter 2)
- Eight-time founder, originally from Cleveland, Ohio
- Dad was CFO of an international industrial company
- Dad and CEO did a leveraged buyout in the 1980s -- **$250k each to acquire a $100 million company**
- Middle-class family; Jeff preferred sports over academics growing up
- Parents valued character over grades: "They were more proud of me for being the person who would befriend the new kid at school than they would have been if I made straight A's."
- Halfway through high school, realized he needed to apply sports discipline to academics
- Undergraduate at **Michigan State University** -- degree in Accounting
- First job at **Ernst & Young** -- completed **500+ bank reconciliations** his first winter
- Moved into E&Y's investment banking group; fell in love with financial modeling
- Earned CPA license, spent four years at E&Y
- MBA at **Harvard Business School**
- Summer internship at **Morgan Stanley's private-equity group** -- received permanent offer
- Wife Linda is a **Moroccan immigrant** -- together 36+ years: "Her grit makes Richard Branson look risk-averse"

### The ERICO Leap (Chapter 2)
- Summer job at ERICO Fastening Systems in New Jersey with HBS roommate **Steve Heese**
- End of summer: CEO asked Jeff to stay as head of Operations and invest in the company
- Already had apartment rented in New York, everything set for Morgan Stanley
- **Turned down Wall Street** -- "I'm sure I was the first person at the Harvard Business School that year to quit my job"
- Borrowed **$100k** from National City Bank in Cleveland using stock as collateral
- Within two months, president was fired -- at age **28**, Jeff became **President of a 300-person, debt-ridden, unionized manufacturing company losing $3M per year**
- Spent **10 formative years** at ERICO
- First 4 years: built state-of-the-art manufacturing plant for copper-bonded ground rods
- Transformed low-margin commodity product into proprietary solution, becoming market leader
- Relocated from Pinehurst, NC to Cleveland, OH -- ran North and South America businesses (8 companies across Canada, Chile, Argentina, Brazil)

### Career Track Record (Chapter 2)
- Across all eight ventures: **raised $212 million, returned nearly $700 million** to investors
- **Median return of 3.3x**
- Five home runs, three strikeouts, 30 years of experience
- "I sometimes wake up in the middle of the night with deep remorse and second thoughts about three of those companies and the investment capital lost"

### The Football Story -- "Raise Your Hand" (Chapter 4)
- Starting tight end for the **Lakewood Rangers** varsity football team (Cleveland, OH)
- Team went **0-10 junior year**; by 8th game of senior year, still hadn't won
- 9th game: Jeff caught 3 passes including a touchdown
- 4th quarter, near opponent's goal line, losing by less than a touchdown
- Quarterback rolled out, primary receiver covered -- Jeff was **wide open in the end zone**
- **Three-second decision:** whether to raise his hand so quarterback could see him
- **He didn't raise his hand** -- afraid he'd drop the ball and get blamed for losing
- Quarterback got sacked, they lost the game
- Team finished with **0-20 record** over junior and senior year
- This decision **haunted him for twenty years**
- "What kind of man doesn't even take a shot at success?"
- Primary emotional driver in high school: **desire to fit in** -- chose mediocrity over risk of failure

### The 2:00 AM Turning Point (Chapter 4)
- At age 38, channel surfing at 2:00 AM
- Found interview with retired C-suite executives asked what they'd change about their careers
- **Every single one** expressed similar regrets: wished they'd been bolder, taken more chances, chosen fulfillment over safety
- "I was far more afraid of mediocrity than of failure"
- Day he decided to take the entrepreneurial plunge to purchase Aztec was the day his youngest son **Jacob** was born
- Family support team: wife Linda, mom, sister Barb
- Mom after a 55-0 football loss: "But I thought you played a really good game, honey!"
- Mom had opened some of the earliest daycare facilities in the 1970s and started the first-ever intergenerational daycare center

### The Companies That Built Jeff (Chapter 3)
- **ERICO (10 years):** Operations, supply chain, cost control, global leadership. "Scaling is not about ideas; it is about building systems that work flawlessly across continents while under pressure."
- **Aztec:** Bought for $13M with $3M seller financing during metal-to-plastic industry shift. Paid down 60% of debt, sold 30 months later for **$21M**. "Momentum favors those who recognize inflection points early."
- **Lynx Professional Grills:** Distressed brand with **25% gross margin** (fatal flaw). Jeff and partners Mike Edwards and Mike Stone redesigned heavy hood with a tiny, inexpensive spring (lift-assist). Managed shift to overseas manufacturing. Transformed economics, sold for **$30M+**, delivering **8.5x return**. "Gross margin determines destiny."
- **Universal Building Products:** Partnered with JH Whitney, acquired/merged two companies for **$150M**. Within weeks of closing: tragic workplace accident claimed a life, federal investigations shut down operations, litigation mounted, non-residential construction market collapsed. Company went bankrupt. "Due diligence is non-negotiable, capital buffers matter."
- **Nika Water:** Social mission brand -- for every bottle sold, funded clean water projects in developing countries. Helped tens of thousands gain access to safe water, but "purpose alone doesn't solve structural business challenges." Introduced Jeff to CPG world.
- **Rowdy Energy:** Energy drink startup. Didn't yield desired outcome but taught what is/isn't essential for building a CPG brand.

## Tactical Advice
- The pattern of choosing action over hesitation creates a habit that makes all the difference when seizing opportunities
- No one can scale on passion or a cool product concept alone -- you need emotional intelligence, financial discipline, great team culture, operational rigor
- Formative experiences compound like interest -- nothing is wasted
- Understanding the language of business (accounting, financial modeling) is foundational
- You don't need decades in a space, but enough exposure to recognize what you don't know
- When asking "Do I have what it takes?" -- reframe to "Am I ready for all that being a founder will require of me?"
